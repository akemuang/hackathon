About the Challenge

The challenge provides a structured opportunity for students to work with real organizational and business problem contexts, select an appropriate technical approach, and deliver a functional project with measurable outcomes.

Core AI Tracks

Teams may focus on one track or integrate multiple tracks where it strengthens real-world applicability.

1) Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Modern organizations generate large volumes of text (messages, reports, reviews, emails, policies). NLP enables systems to analyze and generate language for actionable insight and intelligent interaction.

Typical directions

  • Text classification and sentiment analysis
  • Chatbots and conversational systems
  • Information extraction and summarization
  • Translation or question-answering systems

2) Data Visualization

Visualization helps organizations communicate insight clearly, support analysis, and make evidence-based decisions through well-designed visuals.

Typical directions

  • Exploratory and explanatory visualization
  • Data storytelling and narrative visualization
  • Interactive/static analytics (dashboards, infographics, maps)
  • Complex/high-dimensional/temporal data representation
  • Decision support for planning, operations, policy, or scientific insight

3) Object Detection

Object detection identifies, locates, and classifies objects in images/video for domains such as security, safety, healthcare, retail, logistics, and smart cities.

Typical directions

  • Image/video-based object detection
  • Real-time detection systems
  • Surveillance, safety, healthcare, or smart city applications
  • Integration with IoT or automated decision systems

Expected Outcomes

AIIC 2026 emphasizes working prototypes that can integrate multiple tracks (e.g., NLP + Visualization; Object Detection + Visualization).

Prototype-based, end-to-end functionality

Projects should demonstrate an end-to-end workflow (problem → data → AI processing → interaction → results), and can combine multiple tracks when integration strengthens applicability.

Organizational value and practical use cases

Each prototype should address real enterprise challenges (service quality, operational efficiency, decision support, safety, automation) with a clear value proposition.

Evidence-based results with appropriate metrics

Report measurable outcomes (e.g., accuracy/precision/recall/F1 for models; latency/FPS for detection; usability and interpretability for visualization; business indicators such as time saved or reduced errors).

Implementation readiness and responsible AI

Include system design (data flow, pipeline, interfaces) and demonstrate readiness for deployment, with privacy, security, bias awareness, transparency, and ethical constraints—especially for sensitive or vision-based data.

Evaluation Rounds

Round 1: Walkaround Prototype Evaluation (Top 10)

Purpose: identify teams with strong execution, development efficiency, prototype readiness, and clear progress toward a practical solution.

Round 1 assessment criteria

  • Project Execution & Team Management: roles, workflow, iteration, problem-solving, collaboration
  • Prototype Progress & Development Efficiency: rapid progress, demonstrated pipeline (data → model/logic → output)
  • Technical Implementation Quality: correct use of chosen track(s), stability, structure, reproducibility
  • Clarity of Problem Definition & Relevance: clear enterprise context, users, pain points, expected benefit
  • Testing Evidence & Measurable Results: basic metrics/test cases; proof beyond mock-up

Outcome: Top 10 teams advance. Tie-breaker: Prototype Progress & Development Efficiency, then Technical Implementation Quality.

Round 2: Final Presentation & Prototype Validation (Top 5 Winners)

Purpose: select the most outstanding solutions demonstrating technical completeness, feasibility for real deployment, and strong potential to improve the stated organizational problem.

Finalist format

  • Live demo of the prototype
  • Structured presentation: problem & stakeholders; data & methodology; architecture/workflow; results/metrics; deployment plan & improvements; risks/ethics/limitations

Recommended time allocation

  • 10 minutes presentation + demo
  • 5 minutes Q&A

Round 2 assessment criteria

  • Solution Impact & Business/Organizational Value
  • Technical Completeness & System Reliability (including integration quality if multi-track)
  • Feasibility & Scalability (cost, data availability, constraints, adoption plan)
  • Evaluation Quality & Evidence-Based Results
  • Responsible AI, Ethics, Privacy & Security
  • Communication & Demonstration Quality

Finalists must demonstrate: working prototype (live/runnable), clear problem & target context, at least one measurable result, and ethical/privacy considerations (especially for Object Detection).
