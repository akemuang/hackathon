About the Hackathon

Participants are expected to design, develop, and demonstrate functional prototypes aligned with one or more of the core tracks. Solutions should clearly articulate the problem, methodology, and real-world impact.

Core Tracks

Choose a track that best fits your idea, data, and implementation approach.

1. Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Enable computers to understand, analyze, and generate human language, extracting meaningful insights from unstructured text data or facilitating intelligent human–computer interaction.

Key Focus Areas

  • Text classification and sentiment analysis
  • Chatbots and conversational systems
  • Information extraction and summarization
  • Language translation or question-answering systems

Expected Outcome

A working system that effectively processes natural language data and provides actionable insights or automated responses with clear real-world applicability.

2. Data Visualization

Transform complex datasets into clear, insightful, and interactive visual representations that support decision-making, prioritizing clarity, accuracy, and usability.

Key Focus Areas

  • Exploratory and explanatory data visualization
  • Data storytelling and narrative visualization
  • Interactive or static visual analytics (charts, graphs, infographics, maps)
  • Visual representation of complex, high-dimensional, or temporal data
  • Visual support for decision-making, policy analysis, or scientific insight

Expected Outcome

A visually compelling and user-friendly solution that communicates data-driven insights clearly and supports analysis, planning, or operational decisions.

3. Object Detection

Identify, locate, and classify objects in images or video streams using computer vision techniques, emphasizing accuracy, efficiency, and practical relevance.

Key Focus Areas

  • Image or video-based object detection
  • Real-time detection systems
  • Surveillance, safety, healthcare, or smart city applications
  • Integration with IoT or automated decision systems

Expected Outcome

A functional object detection system capable of recognizing and localizing objects with practical use cases and measurable performance.

General Expectations

  • Solutions must address a real-world problem
  • Projects should demonstrate technical feasibility, innovation, and clarity
  • Clear explanation of the problem, methodology, and impact is required
  • Working prototypes or demonstrable results are strongly encouraged

Tip for Teams

Prepare a concise problem statement, a clear demo flow, and evidence of results (metrics, screenshots, sample outputs) to support judge evaluation.

Selection Criteria After the First Round

After the initial group/team assessment, all participating teams will be evaluated by the judges during an on-site walkthrough and interview session. This stage focuses on practicality, innovation, and clarity.

Selection Process

  1. Judges will walk around the competition area to observe and assess each team’s project.
  2. Each team will be interviewed to explain the problem statement, solution approach, and implementation.
  3. Based on the overall assessment, the Top 10 teams will be selected to advance to the final round.

Final Round

The Top 10 selected teams will deliver a live pitching presentation on stage. Each team must clearly present the idea, solution, impact, and technical approach to the judging panel and audience.
